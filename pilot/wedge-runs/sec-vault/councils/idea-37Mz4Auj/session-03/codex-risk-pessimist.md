That may be true, but it was not sufficiently disciplined about what evidence would distinguish real commercial failure from ordinary annual noise. I did not anchor enough on what would actually have to show up in the numbers: weaker premium-tier mix, margin compression, softer upgrade behavior, or evidence that launches failed to change demand.

If that is the real mechanism, then Pass 1 overstated how much this thesis depends on visible product novelty and understated the resilience of the broader Apple franchise.

## Initial stance (Pass 1)

Twelve months later, this failed because assumption asm-eijc2JDz broke at every load-bearing joint. Apple's R&D spend did not translate into shipped differentiation quickly enough; the integrated hardware-software model became a pacing constraint rather than an advantage; and the resulting products were too incremental to justify premium pricing. The most likely failure path is not a dramatic collapse but a margin-and-relevance squeeze. Competitors matched headline features faster than expected, used lower cost structures to undercut pricing, and trained customers to treat Apple's improvements as non-essential. That turns the core assumption upside down: the integrated stack only helps if it compounds into obvious user value before rivals copy the outcome. If coordination complexity, platform conservatism, or slow standards adoption delayed that payoff, premium pricing became exposed.

A second failure mode is capital misallocation inside R&D itself. Large investments can still earn poor returns if they are spread across too many bets, arrive late, or solve problems customers will not pay for. Once that happens, the downside compounds: gross margins face pressure, product launches look iterative, IP protection is uneven across countries, and fast followers harvest most of the market learning at lower cost. From a risk standpoint, the assumption failed because it required sustained excellence across invention, timing, execution, and pricing power simultaneously; a miss in any one leg was enough to break the result.
