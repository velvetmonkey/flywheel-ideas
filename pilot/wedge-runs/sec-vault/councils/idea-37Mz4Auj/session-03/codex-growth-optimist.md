## Self-critique (Pass 2)

Pass 1 leaned too hard on a familiar narrative that feature parity and pricing pressure naturally erode Apple’s moat. That is plausible, but not sufficient. I did not give enough weight to the counter-case that Apple’s moat is less about headline features and more about distribution, ecosystem retention, silicon efficiency, and trust, any of which could preserve premium pricing even if visible novelty slowed. I also underexplored the possibility that the assumption failed not because innovation weakened, but because investor or customer expectations changed faster than product cycles, which is a different mechanism.

I am also vulnerable to overstating “integration as constraint.” Apple’s control of the stack has historically let it monetize subtle but cumulative quality gains that competitors struggle to replicate at scale. If customer satisfaction, retention, attach rates, and mix remained strong, then my failure story would read as too market-theoretical and not grounded enough in actual buyer behavior.
