## Fragile insights

- Apple's integrated stack is *not* a moat if competitors can integrate faster. The assumption says 'sustains premium pricing'—it doesn't; it sustains *differentiation*. These are not the same.
- Silicon parity ≠ product parity. When Snapdragon matched Apple's perf-per-watt, design and software become the sole deltas. Apple lost the design cycle to Samsung.
- The 'R&D spend yields returns' frame ignores velocity. Competitors now run 12-18 month feature cycles; Apple runs 24. Higher spend at lower velocity = lower ROI.
- The installed base figure (8% growth) obscures cohort churn. New users in emerging markets are buying iPhone 14–15 (older, cheaper SKUs); margin-per-unit is half the flagship baseline.
