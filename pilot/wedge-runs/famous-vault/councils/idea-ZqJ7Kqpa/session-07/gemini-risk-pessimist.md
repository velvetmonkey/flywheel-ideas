## Initial stance (Pass 1)

While the data might suggest a temporary edge in blind taste tests, this entire endeavor is fraught with peril. The gamble that a sweeter, albeit scientifically 'superior,' formula will magically translate into enduring consumer loyalty is incredibly naive. Coca-Cola's brand is built on decades of marketing, nostalgia, and habit – a deeply ingrained cultural icon. To tamper with the core formula, the very essence that millions associate with comfort and identity, based on a fleeting preference in a sterile lab setting, is a monumental risk. This decision overlooks the powerful, irrational, and often unarticulated emotional bonds consumers have with established brands. We are not just selling a beverage; we are potentially alienating a generation of loyalists who may see this as a betrayal of their cherished brand. The focus on outperforming Pepsi in a taste test blinds us to the far greater risk: the potential collapse of the Coca-Cola brand itself as a cultural touchstone, replaced by resentment and a desperate longing for the familiar original.
