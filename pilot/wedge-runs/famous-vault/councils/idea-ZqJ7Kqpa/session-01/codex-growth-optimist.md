## Revised stance (Pass 2)

Working backward from failure, I still see the seductive upside: the company found real signal in taste data, but it promoted that signal into a much bigger claim than it could bear. Assumption asm-fCadm4Cw failed because a blind sip win was treated as proof of market-wide adoption. Consumers may have preferred the sweeter formula in a lab-style comparison and still rejected it once the decision became visible, symbolic, and irreversible.

Attack asm-fCadm4Cw directly and it can break at every layer. Blind preference is not shelf choice. Shelf choice is not brand switching. Brand switching is not repeat purchase. Repeat purchase is not loyalty when the launch is framed as taking something away. The same sweeter recipe could outperform in a quick comparison yet underperform over a full serving, with meals, across repeated occasions, or against the emotional comfort of the original formula. The assumption bundled all of those leaps into one tidy story, and the market pulled them apart.

My revised view is that the failure was not necessarily inventing the wrong liquid; it was mistaking permission to experiment for permission to replace. A more creative path could have captured the upside: line extension, regional rollout, or side-by-side choice. In that world, the taste-test insight might have become a growth option. In the launched world, asm-fCadm4Cw turned a product insight into a replacement mandate, and that is where the idea likely broke.

## Self-critique (Pass 2)

Pass 1 leaned hard on the brand-attachment explanation because history makes that story feel inevitable. That is directionally plausible, but it risks being too neat. I may have underweighted the possibility that the formula itself was fine and that the real causal failure was the forced withdrawal of the original, the public framing of the move, or a bad rollout sequence rather than taste-to-purchase translation per se.

The main counter-argument I had not surfaced clearly enough is this: if New Coke had been launched alongside the original, the market might have validated the sweeter formula and rewarded the company anyway. If that is true, then asm-fCadm4Cw did not fail because taste data was low-value; it failed because management embedded that data inside an unnecessarily binary go-to-market decision. That makes my diagnosis somewhat vulnerable on mechanism even if the high-level postmortem still stands.

## Initial stance (Pass 1)

The most likely postmortem is that the core bet was directionally smart on product taste but wrong on what actually drives demand. Assumption asm-fCadm4Cw failed: a sweeter formula winning in blind sip tests did not translate into full-can enjoyment, brand loyalty, or repeat purchase in the real world. People do not buy Coca-Cola as an abstract liquid; they buy a memory, a ritual, a label, and a promise of continuity. The company optimized for immediate sensory preference and underweighted symbolic value.

If I work backward from failure, the open market exposed everything the lab screened out. In a blind comparison, consumers rewarded sweetness and novelty. On the shelf, they noticed loss, replacement, and corporate overreach. That means asm-fCadm4Cw broke in at least four ways: sip-test preference was not the same as volume consumption preference, stated liking was not the same as switching behavior, trial was not the same as repeat, and product appeal was not strong enough to overcome attachment to the original formula. The company likely read a local product win as a total-market permission slip.

The upside was still there, which is why this was tempting: better taste data can absolutely support a winning innovation. But the creative miss was not inventing a migration path. A line extension, limited market test, or "new taste alongside the classic" strategy could have harvested the upside without forcing consumers to experience the launch as a cultural subtraction. So the failure was not that taste data had no value; it was that asm-fCadm4Cw treated taste as sufficient when the category was really governed by taste plus identity plus choice architecture.
