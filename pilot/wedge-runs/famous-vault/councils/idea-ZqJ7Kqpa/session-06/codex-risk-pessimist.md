## Initial stance (Pass 1)

I oppose this launch as framed. Assumption asm-fCadm4Cw is the entire bridge from lab signal to real-world revenue, and it is a weak bridge. Blind taste tests isolate a sip, not a relationship. They remove brand meaning, habit, identity, meal pairing, repeat-consumption fatigue, and the emotional attachment attached to the existing formula. A sweeter profile can win a short comparison and still lose in sustained, branded purchase behavior.

The deeper failure mode is not merely "the new formula underperforms." It is that replacing the original converts an optimization exercise into an identity breach. Loyal customers are not only choosing a liquid; they are defending a ritual and a symbol. If the company withdraws the original entirely, it gives dissatisfied drinkers no safe fallback inside the franchise and invites them to interpret the change as contempt for their attachment. That creates outrage, media amplification, and organized backlash rather than ordinary trial-and-error product learning.

Even if the new recipe is directionally better in some aggregate taste metric, the downside is asymmetric. A line extension can fail quietly; a replacement of the flagship can fail publicly and force a reversal under humiliation. That damages trust in management judgment, strains bottler and retailer relationships, and teaches consumers that the company will tamper with the core product. The taste-test data is not strong enough to justify that category of risk.
