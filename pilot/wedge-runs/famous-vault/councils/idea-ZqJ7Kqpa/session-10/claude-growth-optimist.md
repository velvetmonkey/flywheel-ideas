## Self-critique (Pass 2)

Pass 1 made three critical errors. First: I treated blind-test data as sufficient for market success, when it's only necessary. Lab preference ≠ real purchasing—that's a separate empirical claim I assumed without evidence. Second: I massively underestimated status quo bias. Beverage choice isn't pure hedonic optimization; it's habit and identity. Coca-Cola drinkers have internalized the brand as self. Switching asks them to abandon loyalty, triggering psychological resistance independent of taste. I also completely missed the discontinuation risk. I said 'trial is automatic' (true), but never grappled with what happens *after* trial when the original is gone. Forced choice between abandonment and acceptance is categorically different from optional upgrade. Third: I overestimated advertising's power against identity. Advertising reinforces preference; it doesn't reverse identity-based resistance at scale. Pepsi's Challenge had already seeded the frame, and New Coke's success on that frame feels like admitting Pepsi was right—a narrative vulnerability I missed.
