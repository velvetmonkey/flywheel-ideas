## Initial stance (Pass 1)

This decision is built on a category error. Assumption asm-fCadm4Cw treats blind sip preference as if it were the same thing as durable market demand. It is not. A branded soft drink is not purchased in a sensory vacuum: consumers buy memory, habit, identity, ritual, and reassurance. A sweeter formula can win in a controlled, short-horizon taste test and still lose badly once people know the flagship product they trusted has been replaced. The failure mode is obvious: management overweights lab-clean preference data and underweights attachment to the incumbent.

The specific downside is not merely that the new formula underperforms. It is that replacing the original formula destroys the recovery path. If consumers reject the change, Coca-Cola will have taught them that the company is willing to discard the core product, provoking betrayal rather than simple disappointment. That converts a product test into a reputational crisis. Because asm-fCadm4Cw assumes stated or revealed blind preference will survive branding, publicity, social discussion, and repeat purchase, it ignores the most fragile link in the chain. My stance is that the launch is materially exposed to backlash, demand erosion, and a humiliating reversal once real-world behavior diverges from taste-test results.
