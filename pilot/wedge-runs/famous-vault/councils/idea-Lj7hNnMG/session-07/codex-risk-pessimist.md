## Initial stance (Pass 1)

On asm-pvzvWzNB: I reject the inference chain. People watching video on phones does not prove premium short-form viewing happens primarily in commute, queue, and break fragments. It may only prove the phone is an available screen. If users switch to TV, laptop, or tablet whenever they have the option, then a mobile-only product does not fit demand; it amputates the higher-comfort contexts where premium video usually wins.

The second weakness inside asm-pvzvWzNB is willingness to pay. Short-form mobile behavior has been trained by free, infinite substitutes and algorithmic discovery. Quibi is trying to charge subscription prices before habit is established, while also denying screen flexibility. That is two adoption taxes at once: pay for it, and only use it on the least forgiving screen.

I also dislike the economics. TV-budget production requires either mass reach or strong retention. This concept threatens both. Limited viewing surfaces, weak shared-viewing dynamics, unclear viral loops, and immediate comparison against YouTube and Netflix create a crowded downside stack. If the in-between-moments thesis is even modestly overstated, the cash burn becomes structural rather than temporary.
