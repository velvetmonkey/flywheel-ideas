## Initial stance (Pass 1)

People do fill commutes, queues, and breaks with short video, but those are fragmented, low-commitment contexts already dominated by free, habitual feeds. I attack asm-pvzvWzNB again on the product side. Even if premium short episodes have appeal on phones, "therefore mobile-only fits" does not follow. Premium video usually wants to move across contexts: phone now, TV later, solo viewing becoming shared viewing when the content is good enough. Mobile-first could have been smart; mobile-only turned a promising format into a narrower and more brittle product rule.

My growth-optimist read is that the upside may have been adjacent, not absent. There may have been a real opening in polished short-form storytelling, but the winning version was more likely cross-device, socially sampleable, and lower-friction on pricing. So I would reject asm-pvzvWzNB as stated while still believing the broader category could have worked in a less doctrinaire shape.
