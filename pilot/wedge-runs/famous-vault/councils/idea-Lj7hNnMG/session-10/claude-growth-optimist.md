## Self-critique (Pass 2)

Pass 1 conflates viewing location with payment psychology. Yes, people watch short-form on mobile — TikTok proved it. No, watching free short-form on mobile does *not* establish that people will pay for premium short-form in the same moment. That's a leap the data never supports. I also undersold subscription saturation: by 2020, the median household already paid $25–35/mo for streaming, and survey data showed willingness-to-add-another-service was collapsing. The 'highest-willingness-to-pay segment' argument assumes that young professionals who subscribe to HBO Max and Disney+ will add Quibi because it's short-form — but they already have short-form via YouTube, Instagram, TikTok, and those services' own short-form properties. The competitive threat isn't Netflix shrinking content; it's free short-form platforms copying the 'premium' label. I also glossed over the app-lock decision as 'correctable,' when in reality locking users out of iPad/web viewing was a feature, not a bug — the entire pitch was 'mobile designed this way.' Removing the lock destroys the narrative differentiation. Timing (COVID) is not my fault, but market saturation and subscription fatigue *were* visible in 2019 and I treated them as cyclical headwinds, not structural headwinds. Finally, I claimed 'habit formation happens in weeks' — true for TikTok and Instagram Reels, which are *free* and *algorithmic*. Quibi is neither. Habit formation for a paywall-first, curated, non-algorithmic service is much slower and weaker. That's the honesty check I missed.

## Key risks

- Subscription willingness assumed from viewing behavior — viewers default to free when free exists; premium requires separate category justification
- Market saturation: 2020 household subscriptions already at $25–35/mo with declining add-rate; Quibi is a tertiary or quaternary add-on, not a core service
- Mobile-only lock-out removes casting, sharing, multi-user household dynamics — designed as feature, functions as handcuff
- Habit formation slower for paywalls + curation vs. algorithmic serendipity; retention curve likely negative month-to-month, not flat
- COVID timing is black swan, but predictable risks (commute loss, churn, burnout budgets) were live in Q1 2020

- Assertion that $5–8/mo is defensible because it's 'lower than Netflix' — ignores the *incremental* price of adding to an existing bundle (Netflix + Disney+ + HBO Max + Apple TV+)
