## Initial stance (Pass 1)

Assume Quibi succeeded and achieved sustainable profitability by Q2 2021. Work backwards. The core assumption holds strongest when we acknowledge what *actually shifted in behavior* by decision-making time and what the 2020 pandemic accelerated. The strongest case: mobile viewing didn't just grow—it fragmented viewing sessions into smaller, discrete units. By 2020, a person's daily video consumption had already split across device, time-of-day, and context: mobile during breaks and commutes (pre-pandemic). COVID-19 intensified this by creating *new* micro-moments at home—phone checks between Slack messages, transitions between Zoom calls, brief scrolls before sleep. The assumption that mobile-first captures this isn't wrong; it's just incomplete without execution. If Quibi nailed content format (5-10 min paced for phone viewing, not desktop), invested heavily in genuinely A-list talent (Spielberg, Sam Esmail), and positioned premium pricing as "better than free TikTok," the service could have defensibly served a real audience. Premium willingness to pay existed elsewhere on mobile (Spotify Premium 100M+ subs by 2020; Apple Arcade launched 2019; Patreon $500M+ revenue). The mistake retrospectively wasn't the assumption—it was that Quibi underestimated that viewing *fragmentation* alone doesn't overcome bigger patterns: people watch premium content when they have *time and attention*, which mobile-only constrains. But if the assumption succeeded, it would have been because the *format* (not the device) became the defensible wedge—short episodes genuinely more rewatchable, citable, shareable than long ones. TikTok proved short-form addiction; Quibi could have proven short-form *premiumbility*. The failure case hinged on misreading whether subscribers would accept mobile-only when pandemic lockdowns gave them desktop/TV time. The success case hinges on whether content quality + format + marketing could have convinced subscribers that mobile *was* the better choice. Charitably, that's not implausible.
