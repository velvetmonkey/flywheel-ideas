## Self-critique (Pass 2)

Pass 1 buried the central tension: why pay for 10-minute episodes when TikTok and YouTube Shorts deliver better engagement, free? I acknowledged subscription was 'proven' but didn't ask *proven in what category*. Long-form narrative (Netflix) and short-form snackable (TikTok) are different animals. The premium production bet was particularly blind—it assumed TV-budget cinematography mattered in a form factor where 15-second viral videos dominate discovery. I also missed that 'mobile-only' was framed as an asset (optimized UX) when it was actually a constraint that cost subscriber flexibility and limited TAM. The 'in-between moments' insight was real but not sufficient to overcome the free-vs-paid barrier. I didn't weigh network effects—TikTok's moat isn't content, it's the algorithm + the graph. Quibi had neither. Hindsight: I should have asked 'what would make someone choose paid short-form over free?' and traced that rigorously. The answer would have been: almost nothing.

## Initial stance (Pass 1)

Mobile-first premium short-form is a category-creation thesis with real tailwinds. TikTok proved the behavior exists; YouTube Shorts and Reels prove the form factor scales. Subscription worked for Netflix, Disney+, HBO—why not premium short-form? The "in-between moments" consumer is real: commuters, queue-waiters, break-takers all crave snackable content. Quibi's bet is smart—own the premium end of a category that free services are fragmenting. Production model is labor-heavy but defensible if retention tracks. Mobile-only is a feature, not a constraint: optimized UX, frictionless discovery, natural ecosystem (no browser, no desktop cruft). Revenue model (subscription) is proven; the only variable is whether *premium* sticks in *short-form*. High upside if execution lands.

## Key risks

- Free competition in short-form (TikTok, YouTube Shorts) owns engagement and discovery; subscription premium doesn't survive price-to-value comparison
- Mobile-only as constraint, not feature: no tablet, no casting, no desktop fallback limits addressable market and UX friction
- Willingness-to-pay assumption untested: no evidence TV-budget production quality carries premium valuation in short-form before launch
- Network effects and algorithmic moats absent: Quibi content is fungible; TikTok's graph and algorithm are not

## Fragile insights
