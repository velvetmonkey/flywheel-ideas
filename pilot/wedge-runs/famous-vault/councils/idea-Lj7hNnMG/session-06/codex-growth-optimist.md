## Initial stance (Pass 1)

I can see the growth logic here. There is a real white space between user-generated snackable video and traditional TV: professionally produced stories that respect mobile attention spans. If Quibi can make "premium in small doses" feel like a new category rather than a compromised one, there is upside in owning a habit that sits between Instagram and Netflix.

That said, I want to attack `asm-pvzvWzNB` directly. I do not think it is safe to assume premium short-form viewing happens primarily in commute-and-queue moments, or that a mobile-only product is therefore the right container. In-between moments are crowded by free, social, algorithmic content, and many of those moments are better suited to low-commitment or audio-first media. The stronger version of the opportunity may be that people want premium short-form in many contexts, including bed, couch, lunch, and second-screen use. If that is true, mobile-first could be smart, but mobile-only becomes an unnecessary constraint.

My optimistic read is that the core demand signal may still exist even if the stated assumption is wrong. The creative alternative is to treat short-form premium video as a cross-context format, not a commute format: start mobile-forward, but allow casting, TV viewing, clips-to-full-episode funnels, and broad social discovery. I would still back the category experiment, but not the narrow behavioral story embedded in `asm-pvzvWzNB`.
