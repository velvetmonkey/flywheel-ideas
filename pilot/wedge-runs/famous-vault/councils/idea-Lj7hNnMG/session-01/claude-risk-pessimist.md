## Key risks

- Confabulation: using COVID pandemic (exogenous) to invalidate a pre-pandemic market hypothesis
- Bundle error: collapsed 'mobile short-form demand exists' (proven by TikTok) with 'premium paid-only mobile-exclusive works' (unproven)
- Comparison fallacy: benchmarked Quibi against Netflix breadth/price instead of YouTube Premium / TikTok positioning
- Execution conflation: Quibi's content-quality, messaging, and landscape-mode capitulation don't prove the assumption was wrong—just that execution failed
- Timing entanglement: March 2020 launch destroys any test of the underlying hypothesis; can't separate 'bad assumption' from 'bad luck'

## Fragile insights

- Claim: 'form factors don't create demand'—but TikTok is a form factor (vertical, short, feed-driven) that *did* create demand. The form factor wasn't the moat for Quibi; the pricing + exclusivity strategy was.
- Claim: 'device lock-in is strategic self-sabotage'—but Apple's vertical-video-optimized ecosystem (e.g., Snapchat Stories) worked. The question is whether *premium* vertical-exclusive can work, not whether vertical video itself is toxic.
- Claim: 'Netflix works everywhere at similar price'—Netflix at $12–15/mo for unlimited catalog is not 'similar' to Quibi at $5–8/mo with 50 shows. Wrong comparison tier.
- Claim: 'COVID didn't kill Quibi; COVID revealed the assumption'—speculative. Pre-COVID commute commuting was weaker than I claimed, but Mar 2020 launch into total lockdown is an unnatural experiment.
