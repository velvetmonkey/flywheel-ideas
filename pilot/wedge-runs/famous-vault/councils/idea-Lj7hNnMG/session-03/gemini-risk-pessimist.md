## Initial stance (Pass 1)

The fundamental premise of Quibi, that users would flock to a mobile-exclusive, premium-priced, short-form video service, was a colossal miscalculation rooted in a deeply flawed understanding of consumer behavior. The assumption that people actively seek *paid* short-form content during fleeting "in-between moments" ignores the reality that these moments are typically filled with quick, free digital distractions or utility-based app usage. Furthermore, the market had already been decisively shaped by platforms like TikTok and YouTube, which offered an overwhelming abundance of free, engaging short-form content, setting a bar that Quibi's premium offering struggled to, or frankly failed to, clear. The "mobile-only" restriction, far from being an innovative feature, was a significant impediment, potentially alienating users who might have engaged with such content on larger screens or wished for more viewing flexibility. This bet on a niche that didn't exist, while free and more compelling alternatives thrived, was a direct path to failure.
