## Self-critique (Pass 2)

Pass 1 made a critical error: I defended the *core idea* by blaming *execution*, but I never actually interrogated the core assumption. I said 'the kernel was right' without proving latent demand for premium short-form exists. The evidence contradicts me: four independent competitors all chose the same model (free + discovery/viral), which suggests that's the equilibrium, not a market gap Quibi could have filled with better ops. I also conflated 'better execution might have extended the runway' with 'the business model was viable.' The capital structure point (can't recoup $1.75B burn) is true but lets me off the hook—the real problem is that short-form as a *format* doesn't support premium pricing because it lacks the switching costs and prestige leverage that work for long-form. I was biased toward 'timing was wrong' over 'the premise was wrong.' COVID timing is convenient cover. The harder truth: even as commutes returned and users had routine, they didn't come back to Quibi. That's evidence the assumption was dead, not dormant.

## Initial stance (Pass 1)

Even in this failure scenario, I see the kernel was right — premium short-form *does* have an audience. But here's where growth vision collides with brutal execution reality. The assumption that 'mobile-only' is a feature, not a limitation, broke us. When COVID lockdowns hit in March 2020, nobody was commuting. The precise behavioral moment we built for vanished overnight. More damning: we locked out iPad and tablet users — alienating the exact cohort with uninterrupted viewing time during lockdowns. Our growth bet was on subscription premium pricing for a format TikTok was giving away free. We wildly underestimated how hard it is to train users to pay *in advance* for short-form when the marginal cost of free alternatives was zero. And the content library? Started too shallow. We needed Netflix's depth with TikTok's velocity and we had neither. I'd bet we could have pivoted to ad-supported free at 500k users and survived. We didn't. The assumption wasn't wrong — the timing and product elasticity were.

- Survivorship of free alternatives: YouTube Shorts launched *after* Quibi died, so treating free short-form as the inevitable equilibrium understates Quibi's first-mover disadvantage
